Hero

Dr. Juan E. Gilbert is an influential American computer scientist, researcher, inventor, and educator known for his work in human‑centered computing and expanding access to technology. He is the Andrew Banks Family Preeminence Endowed Professor and Chair of the Computer & Information Science & Engineering Department at the University of Florida, where he leads research in secure and accessible systems, advanced learning technologies, and human‑centered artificial intelligence. His invention Prime III — an open‑source voting system designed to be accessible to all voters — has been used in elections to improve usability and inclusion, a rare intersection of computing and real‑world impact.

Beyond technical innovation, Dr. Gilbert is celebrated for increasing diversity in computing, receiving the Presidential Award for Excellence in Science, Mathematics, and Engineering Mentoring from President Obama, and being named a laureate of the National Medal of Technology and Innovation. I chose him because his work demonstrates how computer science and software design can solve societal problems and improve accessibility, inspiring my own goals in web programming and ethical tech development.
